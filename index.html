The I Ching will use a flip of three coins per line to determine what goes on each line of your Hexagram. There are six lines in each Hexagram and both are customarily filled in from the bottom to the top. The left Hexagram represents our present and the second our change or future. To visualize, let's pretend each line is a coin-flip Yin Yang:

Two tails and a head will result in a - - Yang unchanged to a future - - Yang. (Black part of Yin Yang)
Two heads and a tail will result in a --- Yin unchanged to a future --- Yin. (White part of Yin Yang)
Three tails will result in a present - - Yin changing to a future --- Yang (The black dot in the white part of the Yin Yang).
Three heads will result in a present --- Yang changing to a future - - Yin (The white dot in the black part of the Yin Yang).
